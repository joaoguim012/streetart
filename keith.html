About Keith Haring


Keith Haring moved to NY to study at the School of Visual Arts and in New York he began making his art. He chose to make his art is public places such as the subway, he would see a small black square in the subway and draw on his many characters in them with chalk. Since his making his art in a public place which was property of the State of New York he was arrested like many street artist before and after him. Unfortunately, Keith Haring was diagnosed with AIDS in 1988, and two years later passed away with AIDS related problems, but his art is still around the United States and many of his art is still located around the world in museums. Keith Haring, a man of the public, made his art not for money, but for the public, most of his art is towards society and his art was mainly focused on the improvement of society and one way he believed he could make the world better was by changing the youth of the country. His art was known for its different figures and colors, and when see a Keith Haring piece know it's his for a reason. Also, Keith Haring was influenced by many great artist, but two artists that heavily influenced him became the centerpiece of one his most famous exhibitions.
